The Pomodoro Technique

Benefits of the Pomodoro technique

The Pomodoro technique is a great way of increasing your focus span and the effectivity of the work being done. Professor Francesco Cirillo wanted to find a way to stop procrastinating and to do his teaching work more efficiently so he came up with the Pomodoro technique.

How to use the pomodoro technique

The implementation of the Pomodoro is that you study in 25-minute intervals and take a 5-minute break between each 25-minute interval, and then take a 15-minute break, depending on how tired you feel, after 4 intervals.
Before starting the Pomodoro, you need to create a To Do Today list. It is crucial to get rid of all the other distraction around you, if in the 25- minute interval you are distracted by someone or something that 25-minute interval should be considered as void.
In the 15- minute break, you should try to do something that gives you more energy to keep going, it could be getting a cup of coffee or taking a walk and etc.
After completing every activity, you should cross it off your To Do Today list.
